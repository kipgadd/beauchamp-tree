If Gottfried Pschigoda and Pauline Stebner are the right couple, this would be the immigrant generation. The surname Pschigoda is most likely a Germanized phonetic respelling of the Polish Przygoda ("adventure") — common in Polish-language sources, often Polish or Polish-Jewish in origin. The shift Przygoda → Pschigoda matches typical Anglo-American or German-Russian transliteration patterns; if this family came in via a German port (Hamburg, Bremen) or via German-speaking territory inside partitioned Poland, the spelling would naturally have Germanized.

The Stebner surname is firmly German, occurring most heavily in Silesia and East Prussia. A Gottfried × Pauline immigrant couple from Silesia or Posen would fit both surnames and would fit the late-19th-century German wave to Michigan that produced so many Berrien County families. None of this is yet confirmed.

V · LOCAL CONTEXT

St. Joseph and Benton Harbor

The whole tree above is anchored to a single small region: the twin cities of St. Joseph and Benton Harbor, Berrien County, Michigan, on the eastern shore of Lake Michigan about ninety miles by water from Chicago. The two towns sit across the St. Joseph River from each other and have been demographically and economically twinned since the late nineteenth century.

St. Joseph itself was platted in 1834 and grew through the late 1800s as a Lake Michigan resort town and shipping point — peach orchards on the surrounding bluffs, paddle steamers across the lake to Chicago, summer cottages on the dunes. Benton Harbor was its industrial twin, settled slightly later as a manufacturing town. In 1911 the Whirlpool Corporation was founded in Benton Harbor — at first as the Upton Machine Company, making electric motor-driven wringer washing machines — and Whirlpool's Benton Harbor headquarters has remained one of the great corporate anchors of the area for a century.

The German and Polish immigrant communities that produced the Pschigodas, Schultzes, and Fehlbergs all settled in this Berrien County industrial belt. Benton Harbor at its peak was a major small-manufacturing town with foundries, machine shops, fruit-canning plants, and (the family-memory thread the user has flagged) various rubber and V-belt operations supplying the auto industry up the lake. The specific employer attaching to William Pschigoda or Fred Fehlberg has not yet been pinned to a documentary record; St. Joseph and Benton Harbor city directories at the Berrien County Historical Association would be the highest-yield next stop.

Water Street Glassworks and the Blossom Fehlberg Metal Studio

The most visible living monument to this family in Benton Harbor is the Blossom Fehlberg Metal Studio at Water Street Glassworks, opened in September 2009 with the support of Nancy Norwood Bowes in honour of her grandmother Blossom Fehlberg (née Pschigoda). Water Street Glassworks itself is a community arts nonprofit running glass-blowing and metal-arts classes; the metal studio was the expansion that brought metalworking into the school's program. It functions, in effect, as a public memorial that anchors the Pschigoda-Fehlberg presence in Benton Harbor across at least three named generations.

★ THE BLENDED-FAMILY READING

Why this tree is "Patricia-centered"

Public obituaries for two of the ten siblings — Linda Carlson-Newell (d. 2017) and Nancy Norwood Bowes (d. 2018) — give different fathers but the same mother. Linda's father is Gordon Carlson Jr.; Nancy's father is Verrill Norwood. Both are children of Patricia Fehlberg Carlson. The full sibling group is therefore best understood as Patricia's children — eight or nine by Gordon Jr. (the published lists are consistent on the names) and one (Nancy) by Verrill Norwood. The Carlson surname has been carried by all ten siblings in adulthood, including Nancy.

The Pschigoda / Przygoda surname

One small linguistic puzzle worth flagging: the family name is consistently spelled Pschigoda in the documents found so far (Patricia Pschigoda Shea's obituary, FamilySearch's record for Marvin "Red" I. Pschigoda, the Geni snippet for William Pschigoda). An earlier theory in the user's working notes was that the original Polish form was Przygoda ("adventure"), a Polish or Polish-Jewish surname identified in surname dictionaries. The shift from Przygoda to Pschigoda is plausible — it's a phonetic respelling appropriate to a German-speaking transit point or to American clerks unfamiliar with Polish orthography — but the two should not be merged in a written family tree until a record actively documents the spelling change. Until then, treat Pschigoda as the correct spelling for everyone in this tree, with Przygoda as a possible older form.

VI · NEXT RECORDS

The five most-useful next documents

Below is the working punch list. Each of these would either lock down a Probable claim, or would extend the tree one further generation back than it currently goes.

Targets · in priority order
  1. William Pschigoda's death certificate or obituary — would confirm or correct the Geni-snippet parents Gottfried & Pauline, and would give a German/Polish hometown that doesn't appear in any of the documents seen so far.
  2. Blossom Pschigoda Fehlberg's marriage record to Fred Fehlberg — Berrien County, MI, probably 1930s. Would conclusively merge the Blossom Pschigoda and Blossom Fehlberg identities with a single document and would fix Fred Fehlberg's parents.
  3. Patricia Fehlberg Carlson's birth or marriage record — Berrien County, MI. Would establish her dates precisely and would tell us whether her marriages to Norwood and Carlson were two formal marriages or a different relationship structure.
  4. Gordon Carlson Jr.'s obituary or death certificate — would name his parents Gordon Sr. and Dorothy explicitly, and would confirm or refute Dorothy's maiden name (the "Dorothy Mae Cubitt" probable from the blocked Find a Grave page).
  5. St. Joseph / Benton Harbor city directories, 1920s–1960s — at the Berrien County Historical Association. Would document William Pschigoda's, Fred Fehlberg's, Gordon Carlson Sr.'s, and Gordon Carlson Jr.'s employers — the highest-yield way to test the family memory of rubber / V-belt / Whirlpool work.

Each of these is a routine pull from public records or local-history archives. Patricia Pschigoda Shea's existing obituary is already the keystone for this tree because it documents the Pschigoda sibling group across both generations; a similarly clear obituary for William Pschigoda or for Gordon Carlson Jr. would complete the symmetric structure.
