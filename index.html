What's been added since the original Lorraine Shoemaker compilation

The original document told a single-spine story: thirty-one generations from Hugh of Bedford down to Kip. This expansion fills out the branches on either side of that spine — the cadet lines and the maternal lines that the original compilation only gestured at.

The senior Earls of Warwick after Guy (gens 9–14) — Thomas the Garter founder, Thomas the Lord Appellant imprisoned in the tower that bears his name, Richard the regent of France and tutor to Henry VI, Henry the Duke of Warwick crowned "King of the Isle of Wight," Anne the wife of Warwick the Kingmaker, Anne Neville the Queen of England, and Edward of Middleham — the only person of Beauchamp blood ever to be heir apparent to the English crown.

The Bletso branch (Margaret Beauchamp of Bletso → Margaret Beaufort → Henry VII) — through which every English monarch since Henry VIII descends. The Powick → Lygon → Madresfield → Brideshead chain, including Hugh Lygon as the model for Sebastian Flyte. The Mortimer descent from your 21× great-grandmother Joan to the Yorkist crown of Edward IV.

The seven maternal lines that built medieval England — Taillebois, d'Abetot, de Braose, Mortimer, Mauduit, Fitz-John, de Toni — each with the major historical figure it brings into the tree.

Edmund Beauchamp the Immigrant's nine children, with documented birthdates and the Turpin marriage in 1692 that begins two centuries of intermarriage with eastern-shore cousins. The Eastern Shore cousinage: Davis, Handy, Hayman, Turpin. Notable later cousins including Fontaine Beauchamp (DAR-recognised Revolutionary patriot), Rev. William Beauchamp (Methodist Episcopal minister), and the infamous Jereboam Beauchamp (the 1825 Kentucky Tragedy).

And on the other end of the line, the five modern surnames — Calhoun, Ansman, Gaddis, Hicks, Carlson — with their etymologies, their geographic patterns, and the late-19th- and 20th-century migrations that brought them together in central Pennsylvania and Vermont.

A separate working page (Maternal Line) covers your mother Beth Carlson-Gaddis's side in detail: the Pschigoda → Fehlberg line out of St. Joseph, MI, the blended Patricia-centered Carlson sibling group, the Norwood-vs-Carlson distinction (Nancy was Verrill Norwood's daughter; the other nine including Beth were Gordon Carlson Jr.'s), and the Water Street Glassworks Blossom Fehlberg Metal Studio in Benton Harbor.
